# Business model & money potential

Arco is **not** a B2C “fun AI tool.” It is **B2B marketing creative infrastructure** for SaaS-shaped buyers (founders are the smallest B2B customer).

## Core category

**B2B Marketing Creative Infrastructure**

More specifically:

- SaaS marketing teams (Phase 2+)
- Indie founders & indie hackers (Phase 1)
- Startup growth / product marketing
- DevTool companies (high aesthetic bar)
- Product launches & feature releases

People don’t casually make promo videos for fun. **Businesses** make them for launches, ads, and conversion.

## Market placement

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| B2C or B2B? | **B2B** (prosumer founder = company of 1) |
| Best model to start | **Model A** — self-serve SaaS |
| Highest ceiling | **Model B** + recurring feature/ad variants |
| Side bet (later) | **Model C** — agencies / white-label |
| Weakest path | B2C + generic “AI video generator” |

## Three business models

### Model A — Self-serve SaaS (start here)

**Who pays:** Founders, indie hackers, 1–3 person SaaS teams.

**Pricing:** $19 → $99/month (tiered by videos/mo + brand kit).

**Value:** Replace Canva + freelance motion designer for launch assets.

| Users | ARPU | MRR |
|-------|------|-----|
| 200 | $30 | $6K |
| 500 | $35 | $17.5K |
| 2,000 | $40 | $80K |

**Why it works:** High-frequency moments (launch, feature, ad variant); clear ROI; fast credit-card sale; matches MVP.

**Risk:** Churn if only “one launch video” — mitigate with changelog + ad templates.

---

### Model B — Launch video engine for funded SaaS (Phase 2)

**Who pays:** SaaS with budget; product marketing; growth teams.

**Pricing:** $100 → $500/month (team seats, unlimited drafts, brand kit, API later).

**Value:** “We generate on-brand launch & feature videos without agency sprints.”

| Companies | ARPU | MRR |
|-----------|------|-----|
| 100 | $150 | $15K |
| 200 | $200 | $40K |
| 500 | $300 | $150K |

**Why it works:** Budget line exists; replaces agency; sticky if tied to release calendar + Figma.

**Unlock:** Team workflow, reliability, case studies, “Stripe-level” output consistency.

---

### Model C — Agency + white-label (underrated, not first)

**Who pays:** Marketing agencies, freelancers, growth studios.

**Pricing:** $50 → $300/month + usage; later $500+ agency tier.

**Value:** Produce client launch packages faster; margin on $1.5k–5k video packages.

**Why it works:** Fewer logos, higher ARPU, multiplies output per seat.

**Timing:** After Model A proves quality; agencies are strict quality judges.

**Practical path:** A → B at ~$50K MRR → C as “Studio / Agency” plan.

## Revenue trajectory (assumptions stated)

**Assumes:** killer demo, URL→MP4 &lt;15 min, 2+ templates by month 6, annual plans, churn &lt;8% on annual.

| Phase | Timeline | MRR | Composition |
|-------|----------|-----|-------------|
| Validation | 0–6 mo | $0 → $2K | 30–80 indies @ ~$25 |
| PMF signal | 6–18 mo | $5K → $20K | 150–400 @ $30–50 |
| Niche scale | 18–36 mo | $50K → $150K | Teams + agencies + ads |
| Stalled | any | &lt;$3K | Wrong positioning or weak output |

**Solo indie ceiling (achievable):** $5K–$25K MRR without sales team.  
**Venture-shaped ceiling:** Model B + ad factory → $1M+ ARR (needs distribution + reliability).

**Exit / strategic interest (rough):** $80K–150K MRR niche B2B → low single-digit to low tens of millions $ interest depending on growth & retention (not consumer unicorn multiples).

---

## Financial risks

1. **Commodity positioning** — “AI video” → $15/mo, churn  
2. **One-and-done usage** — only launch; fix with feature/changelog + ad sizes  
3. **Quality lottery** — one bad export kills PMM trust  
4. **Over-promising AE** — before JSON/plugin exists  
5. **Underpricing** — $9/mo trains wrong market; anchor to freelancer economics
